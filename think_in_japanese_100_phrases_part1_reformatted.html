Getting Started
Use the portal as a guided study path: • Learn phrases in Parts 1–3 • Study characters in Reading Aid and Frequency Deck • Practice in Kana Dojo and Kanji Hub • Follow the 90-Day Tracker and Study Plan • Reduce romaji over time. Small daily practice is more effective than occasional marathon sessions.

Think in Japanese: 100 Phrases, Part 1

Short internal-monologue phrases for building Japanese thought patterns. Reformatted to match Parts 2 and 3: Japanese, romaji, natural English, literal brain translation, and category.

How to use: Don’t memorize every phrase perfectly. Pick 5–10 per day and actually think them during normal moments: waking up, working, driving, eating, waiting, and getting tired.

How to practice

Read the Japanese first, say the romaji out loud, then check the literal brain translation. The goal is to feel the Japanese structure directly.

Daily thinking

These are simple inner-monologue phrases meant for real life: morning, work, tired moments, travel, emotions, decisions, and nighttime.

Literal mode

The “literal brain translation” is intentionally awkward in English so the Japanese word order and logic are easier to remember.
